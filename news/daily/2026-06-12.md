### 3) If You Are Asking for Human Attention, Demonstrate Human Effort

- Track: own_business / better_decisions
- Why it matters to me: ThisHN-posted piece (109 points, 12 comments) articulates a trust principle that applies directly to product positioning and customer acquisition: when you're asking someone to give you their attention or trust, you need to visibly invest effort proportional to what you're asking for. In an AI-saturated market where anyone can generate polished output, the differentiator is no longer quality — it's the visible signal of genuine investment.
- Tradeoff / tension: Signal authenticity vs scale. Demonstrating human effort doesn't scale the way AI-generated content does. But in a world drowning in AI polish, the cost of being perceived as automated may exceed the efficiency gains. The tension is real: doing the visible work is expensive, but not doing it means you're indistinguishable from the noise.
- Implication: For any product or service you build, audit your "attention asks" — every email, landing page, demo request, or outreach message. Wherever you're asking for attention, ask: does the recipient see evidence that I've invested effort specifically for them? If not, you're competing on a field where AI has already won. This is a practical filter for messaging, sales, and product design.
- Action: Review your next three customer-facing messages or product moments. Add one element of visible, non-scalable effort to each. Measure whether response rates change.
- Confidence: medium
- Source: Hacker News front page — https://tombedor.dev/human-attention-and-human-effort/
