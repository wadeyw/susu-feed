### 3) Stripe analyzed 1 million disputes: what actually wins "product not received" cases
- Track: own_business
- Why it matters to me: Stripe's data from one million disputes over 16 weeks reveals counterintuitive leverage points. Full refunds issued through the payment processor add +63 percentage points to win rate; external refunds or store credits add only +6 pp because issuers can't verify them on the card network. Delivery confirmation + GPS map + signature = +44 pp. Submitting evidence after delivery is confirmed yields +27 pp; submitting while in transit yields +2 pp.
- Tradeoff / tension: Refunding through your processor builds trust with issuing banks but costs you revenue. Holding evidence until delivery confirms requires patience and workflow discipline, but the payoff is massive. Specificity beats generality: proving someone streamed or downloaded the exact item sold outperforms proving they had account access.
- Implication: Dispute prevention and evidence collection should be designed into your business workflow from day one, not bolted on when a chargeback arrives. If you sell physical goods, invest in delivery confirmation chains. If you sell digital goods, log specific consumption events. Always refund through your payment processor when possible.
- Action: Act
- Confidence: high
