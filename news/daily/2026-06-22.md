### 2) OpenAI ships enterprise spend controls and usage analytics — the cost layer becomes the product
- Track: own_business
- Why it matters to me: OpenAI is now selling governance, not just capability. The new Global Admin Console unifies credit analytics across ChatGPT and Codex, with granular breakdowns by user, product, model, and group. Employee self-service for credit requests is a subtle but important design choice — it pushes cost awareness onto the user rather than keeping it as an admin-only concern. Zipline's co-founder already cites this as critical for scaling Codex adoption responsibly.
- Tradeoff / tension: Spend controls enable enterprise adoption at scale, but they also introduce friction that can suppress the very experimentation AI thrives on. The tension is between predictability (fixed budgets, group caps) and discovery (unpredictable usage patterns that reveal high-value AI applications). OpenAI's solution — individual overrides + self-service requests — attempts to thread this needle, but the real test is whether it actually reduces waste or just creates bureaucracy.
- Implication: For anyone building businesses that depend on AI infrastructure, the cost-management layer is becoming as important as the model layer. If you're evaluating AI vendors, ask: "What happens when my team's usage grows 10x? Who sees the bill, and what can they do about it?" The companies that solve this internally (with analytics, guardrails, and culture) will adopt AI faster than those that don't.
- Action: Evaluate your own AI spend visibility. If you're running any AI-dependent service or internal tooling, map out who sees usage data, who controls budgets, and how quickly someone can request more capacity. This is an operational risk that compounds.
- Confidence: high
