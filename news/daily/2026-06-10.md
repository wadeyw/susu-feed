### 2) Andrej Karpathy on Jevons paradox and AI software demand

- Track: ai_change
- Why it matters to me: Karpathy is articulating a structural economic pattern that every builder should internalize. As AI lowers the marginal cost of creating software, demand doesn't flatten — it explodes. Explainers, visualizers, dashboards, bespoke single-use apps, hyper-specific wandb instances. The constraint is no longer building software; it's choosing which software is worth building. This directly challenges the assumption that AI makes software cheaper in a linear sense.
- Tradeoff / tension: More capability means more demand for software, not less work. The Jevons paradox means that as AI makes software creation easier, the total amount of software people want grows faster than the cost savings. The tension is between scope discipline (building the right thing) and capability expansion (building more things). Without a strong filter, AI tools can become scope amplifiers rather than leverage multipliers.
- Implication: The competitive advantage shifts from "can build software" to "knows what software is worth building." The skill that compounds is judgment about which bespoke tools, dashboards, and visualizers actually matter — not which ones are technically possible. This rewards people with domain expertise and product intuition over pure coding ability.
- Action: Treat AI coding tools as demand accelerators, not work-reducers. For every new capability you unlock, apply a stricter filter on whether it's worth building. Build a personal decision framework for "should we build this?" that is more rigorous, not less.
- Confidence: medium
- Source: Simon Willison — https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/9/andrej-karpathy/#atom-everything

This makes it easier for smaller players to access frontier capability without the capital burden.
